### Project Overview

This project looks at how solar energy generation in the United States has grown over time. We use a PESTEL approach (mainly political, economic, and environmental factors) and apply a SARIMA model to forecast future values.

The goal is to understand what is driving solar energy growth and make a basic prediction for 2025.

---

### Objectives

* Analyze solar energy data from 2015 to 2024
* Identify key external factors affecting growth
* Explore relationships between variables
* Forecast solar generation for 2025
* Give some general recommendations

---

### Dataset

* Frequency: Monthly
* Period: 2015–2024
* Scope: United States

**Variables:**

* Solar generation (MW)
* Investment (Billion USD)
* Electricity price (USD/kWh)
* CO₂ emissions
* Temperature (°C)
* Precipitation (mm)

---

### Methodology

**1. Data preparation**

* Cleaned the data
* Created time index
* Handled missing values

**2. Exploratory analysis**

* Checked distributions
* Looked at trends and seasonality

**3. Statistical analysis**

* Correlation analysis
* Regression model

**4. Time series modeling**

* Stationarity test (ADF)
* ARIMA and SARIMA models

---

### Forecasting

We used a SARIMA model because the data shows trend and seasonality.

* Forecast period: 2025 (12 months)

---

### Key Results

* Solar generation has increased over time
* There is clear seasonality (higher in summer)
* Investment and electricity prices seem important
* Weather variables affect generation
* Solar growth is linked to lower emissions

---

### Limitations

* Only national-level data
* Short time period
* External shocks not included

---

### Recommendations

* Improve grid infrastructure
* Keep supporting renewable energy policies
* Invest in storage systems
* Use better climate-based forecasting
